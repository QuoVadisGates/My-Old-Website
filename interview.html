Next, round up a team; this team and I would go out to determine the safest routes of travel, once those pathways are determined we'd ask for the people's patience as we help them to their desired destinations.

- What's the most creative way you can break a clock?

A broken clock is still right two times a day, so taking the batteries out or removing anything besides the hands won't be an absolute answer. So I would send 50% of all clocks on Earth into space and send them around the world at thousands of miles per hour. Once the clocks are returned we'll see that because of the theory of relativity's time dilation the clocks on Earth and those sent which have not been physically changed will read different times because the faster you go the more time dialates. Leaving all clock physically perfect yet visually inaccrate.

- You have five bottles with pills. One bottle has 9 gram pills; the others have 10 gram pills. You have a scale that can only be used once. How can you find out which bottle contains the 9 gram pills?

Set the scale to grams place all bottles on the scale at once, it should equal 49 grams. Remove bottles from the scale one-by-one, aiming to subtract 10 with each bottle removed. The bottle that subtracts 9 from the total is the lucky one.
